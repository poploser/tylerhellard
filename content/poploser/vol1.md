I used to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140207092141/http://poploser.org/">blog</a> and then I newslettered [FILE NOT FOUND!] and now I mostly sit around scratching my cat. I miss the internet. 

I’m blogging again, I think. I kind of want to play with it, taking cues from the old versions of Pop Loser, the <a href="https://fimoculous.com">sites</a> I stole from then, and a few <a href="http://www.thingsmagazine.net">sites</a> that have kept on keeping on and I’d like to steal from now. I want it to be weird and esoteric. I want it to be fun and funny. 

The best part: there’s no audience. Zero. Nobody knows this is here and it isn’t plugged into any kind of external social network. I have no tracking or site analytics installed. 

Doesn’t that sound… nice?

### <i class="fa-solid fa-skull-crossbones"></i><i class="fa-solid fa-skull-crossbones"></i><i class="fa-solid fa-skull-crossbones"></i>

<p style="text-align: left;">Will Sasso and Chad Kultgen host a podcast called “Dudesy” with an “AI” also called “Dudesy” because branding is important. That’s about all I know about that (although I was a big Mad TV fan back in the day). The Dudesy dudes then pointed the Dudesy dudebot at George Carlin, creating <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oewO21LQPrQ">an hour-long special</a> [Link comes and goes.] in his style and voice, and thus a fresh can of worms was opened upon this world. 
<p style="text-align: left;">I did not listen to the whole thing, but just enough to be made uncomfortable in the uncanny valley of humour, where you innocently look at the ethereal shape of a familiar(ish) joke and only <a href="https://youtu.be/ML3rNypqHAc?si=yliwqGx6izMD_VXt">lifeless doll-eyes</a> stare back. 
<p style="text-align: left;">And that was the general consensus: this is a bad imitation of a comedy legend. So the <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2024/01/26/arts/carlin-lawsuit-ai-podcast-copyright.html">lawsuit</a> claiming damage to the Carlin’s legacy seems a little specious. ==❝It is a piece of computer-generated click-bait which detracts from the value of Carlin’s comedic works and harms his reputation.❞== If anything, Dudesy has highlighted intangible qualities, some unique to humans, some specific to George Carlin. Call it consciousness, call it genius, call it the soul, there is a void in Dudesy’s impression that is simultaneously difficult to define, but also generally agreed upon. Neat trick, but we listened to George Carlin and you, sir, are no George Carlin. 
<p style="text-align: left;">Legally, this lives in the same grey copyright area as everything else in AI right now. These things are trained on human-created content — dare I say it, “Art” — and then they regurgitate the average of what they learned. As an atheist, I guess I believe every person is the sum total of the things put into them and a person’s ideas are just a manifestation of that, and AI is doing more or less the same thing. I’m not sure I’m quite there yet, though. But still, interesting stuff. All this existential dread over machines that can’t quite understand context or fingers makes for good discourse. 

<p style="text-align: left;">Unfortunately, it’s mostly moot. Under the bright lights of The Legal Process©️, Dudesy the show has conceded that Dudesy the bot is actually just Dudesy the fictional character and the Carlin routine was <a href="https://arstechnica.com/ai/2024/01/george-carlins-heirs-sue-comedy-podcast-over-ai-generated-impression/">entirely written</a> by one of the Dudesy dudes. Weirdly, writing a bunch of jokes that sound like a robot doing an impression of George Carlin is a more artistic endeavour than making a robot do an impression of George Carlin, and they totally nailed it!
<p style="text-align: left;">All of that said, I would probably not be okay if someone I loved was brought back from the dead in the most Black Mirror of ways. Kind of a dick move.

#### What was Vice?
If a tree falls in the woods and someone pulps it into a 90s’ magazine, is that a DOs or a DON’Ts? 

### <i class="fa-solid fa-skull-crossbones"></i><i class="fa-solid fa-skull-crossbones"></i><i class="fa-solid fa-skull-crossbones"></i>

Vice is, for all intents and purposes, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2024/feb/27/vice-clever-irreverent-executives-helped-kill-it">dead</a>. 
<p style="text-align: left;">For much of its history, Vice (the royal Vice—an ever-evolving magazine thing and website thing and news thing and video thing and brand thing and aesthetic thing) seemed both outside and ahead and irreverent, but also reckless and gross and, well, <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/video/business/100000003509105/page-one-david-carr-confronts-vice.html">douchebaggy</a>. I loved Vice, even if they did seem singularly dedicated to never quite being the <a href="https://deadspin.com/the-adults-in-the-room-1837487584">adult in the room</a>. 
<p style="text-align: left;">I went to university in a small town in eastern Nova Scotia. We did not get Vice. If you were lucky (and happened to be in Halifax), you could get one at <a href="https://www.thecoast.ca/news-opinion/the-paperchase-closes-next-week-4588484">Blowers Street</a> before they sold out. Occasionally I’d get some back issues sent to me via an informal network on a Canadian University Press listserv. 
<p style="text-align: left;">Here’s a screenshot of my first ever Amazon order: 
<img src="https://tylerhellard.com/uploads/2024/screen-shot-2024-02-29-at-11.30.49-am.png" width="600" height="507" alt="">
<p style="text-align: left;">That book was in the bathroom of me and my wife’s first apartment for five years, so I read it a lot of times, at least until iPhones got really good. A few years ago, I met <a href="https://www.vice.com/en/contributor/mack-lamoureux">Mack</a> at a thing and told him I thought his dives into the right-wing extremes of Canada was the most important journalism happening. Vice was really cool! 
<p style="text-align: left;">But <a href="https://pluralistic.net/2024/02/24/anti-posse/#when-you-absolutely-positively-dont-give-a-solitary-single-fuck">also</a>: ==❝Vice died the way it lived: being suckered in by smarter predators, even as it trained its own predatory instincts on those more credulous than its own supremely gullible leadership.❞== Well, yeah. There’s that. And <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gavin_McInnes">that</a>, too. This is a <a href="https://newrepublic.com/article/179276/vice-magazine-history-layoffs-digital-media-bubble">pretty a comprehensive accounting</a>. It ain’t great. History is messy or something.
