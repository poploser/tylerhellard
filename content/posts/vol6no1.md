#### No good read goes unpublished.
*Vol. 6, No. 1* 

<img src="https://res.cloudinary.com/dwwbmrctk/image/upload/q_auto/f_auto/v1781926087/Pop_Loser_Vol._6_No._1_ocxo8i.png" alt="Pop Loser Vol. 6, No. 1" class="art">

I’ve had a very strange three months where a little novel I wrote nearly a decade ago suddenly became a Canadian best seller, sending me onto <a href="https://www.cbc.ca/listen/live-radio/1-77-bookends-with-mattea-roach/clip/16198775-meet-hockeys-greatest-fictional-goon">radio programs</a>, TV and live events around the country, and it was all thanks to a beautiful man called "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/@stevedangle">Dangle</a>." An unexpected and incredible thing. (Spoiler: we didn’t win.)

I’m a CBC junkie, so I was already a fan of <a href="https://www.cbc.ca/books/canadareads">Canada Reads</a>. If you aren’t, now is as good a time as any. Someone over at <a href="https://openlibrary.org/collections/canada-reads">Open Library</a> just put together a collection of all the show’s books from the last decade, with plans to keep going. Dig in. 

But I guess we’re back to this now. Much as I am grateful for the experience of the last few months, sending things out into the ether from here is a scale I’m more comfortable with. 

Speaking of comfort levels, after spending a few years pulling myself off of online platforms and social media, having swaths of the country talking about my book got me back on and I (re-)developed a pretty unhealthy relationship with vanity searches and Goodreads reviews and something called BookTok. Now I have to detox my bad habits (and my ego) all over again. The internet remains a relentless motherfucker and I hope we just decide to turn the whole thing off one day. Except email. Email is cool. 

Welcome to Pop Loser Volume 6. ✌️
